# How I Built My Portfolio Website: A Simple Journey

*From idea to live website - the story behind creating a modern portfolio that anyone can understand*

---

## Why I Decided to Build My Own Website

As a Mendix developer working at Deloitte, I realized I needed a better way to showcase my work and connect with potential clients and employers. While there are many website builders available, I wanted something unique that truly represented my skills and personality.

So I decided to build my own portfolio website from scratch. Here's how I did it, explained in simple terms that anyone can understand.

## What I Wanted to Achieve

My goal was simple: create a website that would:
- **Show my projects** in an attractive way
- **Share my thoughts** through a blog
- **Display testimonials** from people I've worked with
- **Make it easy to contact me**
- **Work perfectly on phones and computers**
- **Let me update content easily** without touching code

## The Tools I Chose

Think of building a website like constructing a house. You need different tools for different parts:

### The Foundation (Next.js)
I chose Next.js as my foundation because it's like having a really good construction crew. It handles all the complex stuff automatically, making websites fast and reliable.

### The Database (Supabase)
For storing information like blog posts and projects, I used Supabase. Think of it as a super-organized filing cabinet that can instantly find any information I need.

### The Design (Tailwind CSS)
For making everything look beautiful, I used Tailwind CSS. It's like having a professional interior designer who can quickly style any part of the website.

### The Hosting (Vercel)
Finally, I used Vercel to put my website on the internet. It's like having a reliable landlord who makes sure your house is always accessible to visitors.

## The Building Process

### Step 1: Planning the Layout
Before writing any code, I sketched out what I wanted:
- A welcoming homepage with my photo and introduction
- A projects section showing my best work
- A blog where I can share insights
- A testimonials area for client feedback
- An easy way for people to contact me

### Step 2: Creating the Admin Panel
One of the coolest features I built is an admin panel - think of it as a control room where I can:
- Add new blog posts without touching code
- Upload project images and descriptions
- Approve testimonials before they go live
- Turn different sections of the website on or off
- Reset my password if I forget it

### Step 3: Making It Interactive
I added several interactive features:
- **Dark and light themes** - visitors can choose what's easier on their eyes
- **Smooth animations** - everything moves gracefully when you scroll or click
- **Like and comment system** - people can engage with my blog posts
- **Image uploads** - testimonials can include photos
- **Email notifications** - I get notified when someone submits a testimonial

### Step 4: Mobile-First Design
Since most people browse on their phones, I made sure everything works perfectly on small screens first, then enhanced it for larger screens.

## The Features That Make It Special

### Smart Content Management
Unlike static websites, mine is dynamic. I can:
- Write blog posts with rich formatting (bold text, images, links)
- Add new projects with detailed descriptions
- Control which sections visitors see
- Update content instantly without rebuilding the site

### Professional Blog System
The blog isn't just a simple page - it includes:
- Like buttons for each post
- Comment system with moderation
- SEO optimization so people can find my posts on Google
- Social sharing capabilities

### Testimonial System
People can submit testimonials with:
- Their photo (or I can use their LinkedIn photo with permission)
- Star ratings
- Detailed feedback
- Admin approval before going live

### Email Integration
The website can send emails for:
- Password reset requests
- New testimonial notifications
- Contact form submissions

## Challenges I Overcame

### Making It Fast
Websites need to load quickly or people leave. I solved this by:
- Optimizing all images automatically
- Loading content only when needed
- Using modern web technologies that are naturally fast

### Keeping It Secure
Security was crucial since I have an admin panel. I implemented:
- Secure login system
- Protected database access
- Input validation to prevent malicious content
- Encrypted data transmission

### Making It User-Friendly
I wanted both visitors and myself (as admin) to have a great experience:
- Intuitive navigation that works on all devices
- Clear visual hierarchy so important information stands out
- Smooth animations that guide the eye
- Easy-to-use admin interface

## The Results

After several weeks of development, I launched a website that:
- **Loads in under 2 seconds** on most connections
- **Works perfectly on all devices** from phones to large monitors
- **Ranks well on Google** thanks to proper SEO
- **Converts visitors to contacts** with clear call-to-action buttons
